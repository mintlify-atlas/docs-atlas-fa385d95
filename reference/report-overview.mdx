## Overview

This project ships two PDF research reports that document a comprehensive reverse-engineering of a production AI coding agent's product design.

| Report | Description |
|---|---|
| `ai-agent-deep-dive.pdf` | Version 1 — initial architectural analysis |
| `ai-agent-deep-dive-v2.pdf` | Version 2 — updated and extended analysis (latest) |

<Note>
The v2 report is the current, complete version. Start here if you are reading the research for the first time.
</Note>

## What the reports cover

The reports reconstruct the product requirements and architectural decisions of a production AI coding agent runtime from a product manager's perspective — without leaking implementation source code.

## Reading order

If this is your first time reading the research:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Product overview">
    Start with `00-product-overview` to understand what the product is trying to achieve and why simpler approaches fall short.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Memory and session">
    Read `04-memory-and-session` — memory architecture is one of the most underappreciated design challenges.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tools and permissions">
    Read `02-tools-permissions-and-execution` to understand why a governed tool pipeline is essential.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Skills, plugins, and MCP">
    Finish with `03-skills-plugins-mcp` and `06-verification-and-quality` for the extensibility and quality assurance layers.
  </Step>
</Steps>
